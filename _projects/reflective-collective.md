**The Reflective Collective** is a faculty community of practice built on
one simple premise: the teachers who grow most over time are the ones who
keep asking themselves better questions.

## The design move

Most faculty development is transactional — a workshop, a handout, a quick
fix. The Reflective Collective deliberately runs at a different tempo.
Members commit to a semester-long arc built around Appreciative Inquiry
cycles: discovery, dream, design, destiny. Meetings are structured but
unhurried; the facilitator's job is to protect the quiet, not fill it.

## Format

- Small cohorts (8–12 faculty) drawn across disciplines
- Bi-weekly 90-minute sessions
- Each session anchored by a member's real teaching puzzle
- Structured reflection protocols — no generic "what worked, what didn't"

## What I'm learning

Slower programs produce different artifacts. Members leave with
*questions*, not *tips* — and report changes in how they plan courses
months later.
